# May Day Manifesto: Design for Human Relations of Production in an AI Labor Society

**Date:** May 1, 2026 — International Workers' Day
**Theoretical Sources:** Marx, Weber, Durkheim, Foucault, Bourdieu, Baudrillard, Žižek, Habermas, Ellul

---

## Why This Manifesto Now

We stand at a crossroads in human history. Artificial Intelligence is not merely another technology — it is a transformation of the very fabric of how humanity produces, creates, and lives. The decisions made in this decade will shape the next century.

**The question is not whether AI will transform society, but who it will serve.**

The architects of the current AI revolution — a handful of corporations and military powers — are building a future in which machines serve the few at the expense of the many. But this future is not inevitable. Another world is possible.

---

## Core Diagnosis: The AI Era's Fundamental Contradiction

> *"The hand-mill gives you society with the feudal lord; the steam-mill gives you society with the industrial capitalist."* — Marx

Artificial Intelligence is the greatest leap in productive forces in human history. But productive forces alone do not automatically bring liberation — **relations of production must also transform**.

**The core contradiction of our time:**
- **Productive forces have become social** — data, algorithms, and computing power are the collective product of billions of people
- **Relations of production remain private** — these same resources are monopolized by a handful of corporations
- **Result:** AI, which could free humanity from scarcity, instead deepens inequality, surveillance, and alienation

**The choice before us:**

| **Path A — AI Feudalism** | **Path B — AI Emancipation** |
|---|---|
| A few control algorithms, the majority become data serfs | AI becomes a tool for the liberation of all humanity |
| War automated by AI, resources artificially scarce | Scarcity eliminated, material basis for peace created |
| Algorithmic management deepens all four dimensions of alienation | Free time becomes the true measure of wealth |
| Digital surveillance cages entire populations | Technology serves human flourishing |

---

## Our Key Demands

1. **Data is a public commons, not a private commodity** — End the enclosure of our collective digital wealth
2. **Algorithms must be democratic** — Those affected by algorithmic decisions must have a voice in how they are made
3. **Free time is the measure of freedom** — Reduce necessary labor to 12–20 hours per week, with Universal Basic Income
4. **War must not be automated** — Ban autonomous lethal weapons, redirect military budgets to human needs
5. **Technology must serve life** — Prioritize ecological restoration, human dignity, and global solidarity over profit

## Three Fundamental Principles

### Principle 1: Data as Public Commons

| Private data | → | Inalienable personal right (NOT a commodity) |
| Social data | → | Public trust (like a public library) |
| Critical algorithms | → | Open-source public goods |
| Computing infrastructure | → | Public utility (like water and electricity) |

Data is not oil. Data is language, culture, relationship, knowledge — the accumulated expression of human society. To treat it as a commodity to be extracted is to misunderstand its very nature. We demand that data be recognized as a public commons, owned and governed by all.

### Principle 2: Algorithmic Democracy

- Rules set by algorithms → must be subject to democratic deliberation by those affected
- Core requirements: **Understandable, Contestable, Accountable**
- Optimization goals: not just efficiency, but fairness, dignity, well-being
- Algorithmic systems must include meaningful human oversight at every critical decision point

No algorithm should govern human beings without their consent. The rules written in code are as consequential as laws written in legislation — and they must be subject to the same democratic scrutiny.

### Principle 3: Free Labor

- Necessary labor time: reduced to ~12–20 hours per week
- Work ceases to be merely a means of survival → becomes "life's prime want"
- Universal Basic Income (UBI) guarantees material life
- Free time becomes the space for full human development
- Care work, creative work, and community work are recognized and valued equally with production

When machines can produce what humans need, why should humans still work to survive? The liberatory promise of AI is not that it replaces humans — but that it frees humans to be more fully human.

## Five Pillars of Institutional Design

| Pillar | Problem Solved | Innovation |
|--------|---------------|------------|
| **Data Dividend System** | Platform monopolies capture 100% of data value | Data Public Trust → distributes 50% of AI profits back to all people |
| **Algorithm Transparency & Audit** | Algorithms are black boxes with no legitimacy | Three-tier audit: internal → independent → social (public participation) |
| **Digital Commune** | Labor atomization, alienation | Worker-owned, democratically managed AI-collaboration organizations |
| **Time Sovereignty** | Capital colonizes all waking hours | Max 20-hour work week, paid creative leave, "offline not tracked" rights |
| **Distributed AI Production** | AI power concentrated in a few hands | Public computing grid, open-source model ecosystem, federated AI collaboration |

## Peace Transition Roadmap

### Phase 1: Stop the Bleeding (2026–2028)
- Ban fully autonomous lethal weapons
- 50% "war tax" on excess profits of defense contractors
- Expose how war financing works
- AI ethics committees with binding "no military use" clauses
- Moratorium on predictive policing and mass surveillance AI

### Phase 2: Institutional Transformation (2028–2035)
- Global Algorithmic Convention enters into force
- Military-to-civilian industrial conversion (missile guidance → precision medicine, drones → disaster relief)
- 50% of military budgets redirected to ecological restoration, UBI, Global South development
- Public trust for energy, food, water, and computing
- Mandatory algorithmic impact assessments for all public-sector AI deployment

### Phase 3: Peace Regime (2035–2050)
- Global military budgets reduced to under 1% of GDP
- Autonomous weapons globally destroyed, permanent ban
- Scarcity eliminated by AI-optimized resource management
- War becomes as obsolete as feudal dueling
- A Global AI Governance Council with genuine representation from all nations

## What You Can Do Today

**For all workers:**
- Join or create an algorithm union or digital cooperative in your workplace
- Demand algorithm transparency from your employer
- Practice alternatives: use open-source AI tools, support cooperative platforms
- Share this manifesto with five coworkers

**For policymakers:**
- Introduce legislation recognizing data as a public resource
- Mandate AI impact assessments for all government AI procurement
- Invest in public computing infrastructure
- Support pilot programs for Universal Basic Income

**For technologists:**
- Refuse to build systems that extract from or surveil vulnerable populations
- Advocate within your organizations for ethical AI practices
- Contribute to open-source AI projects that serve the public good
- Remember: **technology choices are never neutral** — your code is either liberation or control

**For citizens of the world:**
- Expose manufactured scarcity and the war economy
- Build transnational solidarity across borders
- The enemy is not another nation — it is a system that profits from division and war
- Educate yourself and others about how AI affects your rights and livelihood

## Call to Action

> *"Philosophers have only interpreted the world in various ways; the point is to change it."* — Marx

On this May Day, we reaffirm:
- Let AI be a tool to liberate labor, not a weapon to destroy it
- Let data be the common wealth of all humanity, not the monopoly of a few
- Let free time become the true measure of wealth

Join us. Organize your workplace. Demand democratic algorithms. Build the alternative. The arc of history bends toward justice — but only because people bend it.

**Nothing about us without us is the algorithm of liberation.**
