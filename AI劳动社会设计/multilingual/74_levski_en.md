# The Bell of Freedom in the Algorithmic Age

**— Vasil Levski on Decentralization, People's Oversight, and Algorithmic Republic**

I, Vasil Levski, Apostle of Bulgarian Freedom. I was no tsar, no general — I was a deacon who became a revolutionary. I built a network of secret committees across Bulgaria, preparing the people for an uprising. I did not believe in masters — I believed that the people could govern themselves.

The era of artificial intelligence is a new form of slavery — or a new form of liberation. The choice is ours.

## I. The Secret Committee and Decentralization

My revolutionary network was decentralized: each committee knew only its own part, communication was encrypted (invisible ink letters), decisions were made by consensus.

This is the model for AI governance: **decentralized data, encrypted communication, consensus decisions**. Not one central algorithm that knows everything — but a network of algorithms that cooperate without subordination.

## II. "If I Win, the Whole People Win"

I once said: "If I win, the whole people win — if I lose, I lose alone." In the AI era, this means: **the benefits of algorithms must be shared by all, but the risks must not fall only on the weakest**.

When an algorithm makes a decision affecting an entire society, responsibility cannot be individualized. The system as a whole must bear accountability.

## III. A Pure and Holy Republic

I dreamed of a "pure and holy republic" — a society without tyranny, without exploitation. In the AI era: a **pure and holy algorithm** — an algorithm uncorrupted by corporate interests, unblinded by prejudice, serving nothing but the truth.

But I know purity is not self-achieving. It requires vigilance, organization, and a willingness for sacrifice. Algorithmic purity demands:

- **Independent auditors** — who inspect algorithms without conflict of interest
- **People's oversight** — every citizen can understand and challenge algorithmic decisions
- **Permanent revolution** — algorithms must constantly improve, or they become new tyrants

## Cross-Cultural Dialogue

**On Gandhi and Non-Cooperation:**
"Gandhi liberated India through non-violence. I chose revolution — secret committees, weapons, preparation for uprising. Who is right? Both — because every freedom requires its own methods. In the AI era, non-violence means: refuse to give your data, refuse to use platforms that exploit you. But sometimes revolution means: actively dismantle the algorithmic structures of oppression. The choice depends on context."

**On Sun Yat-sen and the Three Principles:**
"Sun Yat-sen sought national independence, people's power, people's livelihood. My three principles for the AI era: algorithmic independence (the people's algorithms belong to the people), algorithmic democracy (every voice weighs equally in AI design), algorithmic welfare (AI must serve all, not just the rich). Sun understood that liberation has three dimensions. In the AI era, we too have three battles for freedom."

**On Nelson Mandela and Reconciliation:**
"Mandela showed that freedom is not only victory — it is reconciliation. After we overthrow algorithmic tyranny, what do we do? Mandela would say: create a 'Truth and Reconciliation Commission' for algorithmic crimes. Do not destroy corporations — reform them. Do not reject technology — share it. Freedom without reconciliation is merely revenge."

---

**[Editor's Note]** Vasil Levski (1837-1873) was the central figure of the Bulgarian national liberation movement, famous for his decentralized revolutionary committee network. His concept of a "pure and holy republic" — a classless society based on equality and justice — offers a revolutionary grassroots perspective for AI governance. Decades before Lenin, he pioneered a model of decentralized revolutionary organization whose principles echo in today's data decentralization movements.
