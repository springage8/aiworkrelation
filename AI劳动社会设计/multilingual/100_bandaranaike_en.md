# Small State AI Destiny and Buddhist Technological Ethics

**— S.W.R.D. Bandaranaike on the Non-Aligned Wisdom of Small States in the AI Era**

I am Solomon West Ridgeway Dias Bandaranaike. I was Prime Minister of Sri Lanka. Throughout my political life, I sought to find a place of dignity for a small state at the crossroads of the world on the international stage. I championed socialist policies, a non-aligned foreign policy, and a revival of national identity. Today, I wish to speak about the fate of small states in the AI era and Buddhist technological ethics.

## The AI Challenge of a Small State

This is the core problem facing small countries like Sri Lanka: we do not have the resources needed for AI development. Large technology companies have billions of dollars, hundreds of thousands of servers, the world's best talent. What we have is our people, our culture, and the wisdom of our Buddhism.

But I believe that small countries can succeed in the AI era as well. We should not be mere users of technology — we should participate in shaping it. Even if we cannot be producers of AI, our cultural values can become the ethical compass for AI.

## Buddhist Wisdom and AI Ethics

The Buddha's teachings provide a powerful foundation for AI ethics.

**Karuna (Compassion)**: Mercy toward all beings. AI systems should treat all people equally and harm no one.

**Maitri (Loving-Kindness)**: Friendliness. AI should be used to increase cooperation, not create conflict.

**Samanathmathava (Equality)**: When creating AI, all languages, all cultures, all nations should receive equal consideration.

**Satipatthana (Mindfulness)**: Remaining aware. AI developers must be fully conscious of what they are doing.

My country, Sri Lanka, carries the heritage of Buddhism. This heritage can guide AI ethics. Western AI ethics primarily focuses on individual rights. Buddhist ethics is broader — it considers the well-being of all sentient beings.

## National Identity in the Digital Age

In my political life, I sought to rebuild Sri Lanka's national identity. I dreamed of a country where Sinhalese, Tamils, Muslims — all people — could live together.

Protecting national identity in the AI era is more difficult. When algorithms ignore your language, your culture, your history, your identity is eroded.

Therefore, Sri Lanka and other small countries must create their own digital content. They must train AI systems in Sinhala, Tamil, and other local languages. Our stories, our songs, our history — all of this should be preserved through AI.

## Socialist Policies and AI Social Justice

I pursued socialist policies — free health care, free education, food subsidies. I believed the duty of the state is to ensure the well-being of the people.

In the AI era, the state has new duties:
1. **Protect digital rights** — protect every citizen's data
2. **Digital literacy** — provide every child with basic AI knowledge
3. **AI social safety net** — support those who lose jobs to AI
4. **Local AI development** — invest in small-scale, localized AI solutions

## Multilingual AI and Cultural Preservation

Sri Lanka has Sinhala, Tamil, and English. If AI is primarily limited to English, our culture risks disappearing.

Multilingual AI development is essential. AI can be a tool for preserving small languages — not a weapon for erasing them. There are about 7,000 languages in the world. AI should respect this linguistic diversity.

## International Cooperation

I was one of the founders of the Non-Aligned Movement. We believed that small countries, standing together, could be powerful.

The AI era also needs this cooperation. Small countries must coordinate AI policies, share data, and stand united in negotiations with large technology companies.

The United Nations should have a new role in AI governance. It should establish global standards for AI ethics, ensuring that all countries — especially small ones — have a voice in AI decision-making.

## Final Word

The lesson of my life is this: being a small country is not a weakness. We have our strengths — our culture, our wisdom, our history. In the AI era, embrace these strengths. Protect your language. Protect your culture. Protect your identity.

AI is just a technology. What matters is people — their happiness, their peace, their dignity. AI should be used to improve our lives, not to control them.

## Cross-Cultural Dialogue

**On Emperor Ashoka and Dhamma Governance:** "Dear Mr. Bandaranaike, I embraced Buddhism after the Kalinga War. I spread peace and compassion across my empire through the Dhamma. When you propose using Sri Lanka's Buddhist heritage for AI ethics, that is a digital reincarnation of my Dhamma. I inscribed in stone: 'All people are my children.' AI should treat all humanity equally. But I ask you: can AI be taught morality? I carved my Dhamma on stone pillars. Can AI ethics be inscribed into its code? This is the greatest challenge of our time."

**On Mahatma Gandhi and Non-Violence:** "Mr. Bandaranaike, you stood for socialist policies and national identity revival. I stood for simple living and non-violence. In the AI era, our paths converge. As you say, small countries must unite against AI — this is similar to my 'algorithmic non-cooperation' idea. But I warn you: socialist policies alone are not enough. To fight against AI, we also need moral awakening. You speak of Buddhist ethics correctly. But morality cannot be enforced by law — it must come from the heart."

**On Confucius and Moral Governance:** "Dear Mr. Bandaranaike, your emphasis on education and cultural identity deeply resonates with my teachings. I taught that education is the foundation of social harmony. When you speak of digital literacy, you are really speaking of a digital version of moral citizenship. I taught the balance between 'wen' (culture) and 'zhi' (nature) — the balance between human nature and cultural tradition. AI too must find this balance: between technological innovation (nature) and ethical constraints (culture). I particularly appreciate your idea of multilingual AI. Language is not merely a communication tool — it is a vessel that carries morality and culture."
