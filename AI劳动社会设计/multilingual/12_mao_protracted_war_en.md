# On Protracted War in the AI Era

> Knowledge distilled from *On Protracted War* (1938)
> Date: May 2, 2026
> Theoretical sources: Mao Zedong, Marx's *Capital*

---

## I. Core Thesis

> **"War is the continuation of politics."** — Clausewitz

The same applies to transformation in the AI era. It is not a technology upgrade—it is a protracted political struggle over the ownership of data as means of production.

This document refutes **two erroneous views**:

| View | Claims | Error |
|------|--------|-------|
| **Speed victory** | "AI is developing so fast, capital will collapse on its own—we just design the institutions and wait" | Underestimates capital's adaptability. Capital can absorb criticism, make concessions, and shift contradictions to prolong its life. |
| **Doom** | "AI monopoly capital already controls computing, data, models, media, government—we can't change anything" | Exaggerates enemy strength, ignores the development and transformation of contradictions. Every major historical transformation looked "impossible" at the start. |

**Core judgment of this document:** Transformation in the AI era is a **protracted war**. It will neither achieve quick victory nor end in defeat—provided we adopt correct strategy.

## II. Comprehensive Analysis of Forces

### 2.1 Enemy Advantages

AI monopoly capital currently holds overwhelming force superiority:

```
Computing advantage:
  90%+ of global AI training compute concentrated in fewer than 10 companies
  Training a large model (e.g. GPT-5) costs billions of dollars
  Barriers rising—data common servers can't run these models

Data advantage:
  User behavior data locked on platforms—"data moat"
  More data → better models → more users → more data — positive feedback loop

Talent advantage:
  Top global AI talent locked in by high salaries
  In 2025, Google, Meta, OpenAI, Microsoft employed 70%+ of AI PhD graduates

Policy advantage:
  In major countries (US, China), AI policy heavily influenced by big tech lobbying
  EU's AI Act is a partial exception but enforcement remains questionable

Ideological advantage:
  "Technological neutrality" — AI appears as "objective tool" not power structure
  "Innovation first" — any regulation portrayed as hindering progress
  "Market choice" — consumers "freely choose" to use platforms, but have no real alternative
```

### 2.2 Our Advantages

The strength of forces for change lies not in "now" but in **the developmental trend of contradictions**:

```
Contradiction 1: Profit maximization vs purchasing power
  AI replaces workers → unemployment rises → purchasing power falls → AI product market shrinks
  Capital cannot destroy consumers while maintaining profits
  → This contradiction is structural and irreconcilable

Contradiction 2: Private data vs social nature of data
  Data maximizes value only when it flows across all of society
  But monopoly enclosure "imprisons" data on platforms
  → Private ownership increasingly fetters productive forces
  → Data common ownership is more efficient—not a moral judgment, an economic law

Contradiction 3: Global AI capital vs national sovereignty
  AI crosses borders, but the nation-state remains the basic unit of political power
  → More and more countries (EU, China, Latin America, India) will take measures to limit AI monopoly
  → Not because "they support common ownership," but because "they don't want to be ruled by foreign capital"
  → But this force can be utilized by us

Contradiction 4: Expanding AI replacement
  More people replaced by AI → more dissatisfaction → more potential transformative force
  When replacement rate passes from "10% unemployment" to "30% unemployment" — systemic legitimacy crisis arrives
```

### 2.3 Comprehensive Assessment

```
Current stage: Enemy strategic advantage
  → Enemy holds initiative, we are on defense
  → Main task is not to attack, but to accumulate strength

Middle stage: Stalemate
  → Data common ownership experiments prove feasibility
  → AI replacement unemployment reaches critical threshold (est: 15-25%)
  → Social contradictions intensify, middle begins to shift
  → Forces form a stalemate

Long-term stage: Strategic counter-offensive
  → AI monopoly capital's production relations can no longer contain productive forces
  → Data common ownership proven economically superior to private ownership
  → Political conditions mature, systemic transformation begins
```

## III. The Three Stages in Detail

### Stage 1: Strategic Defense (Present — ~2030)

**Situation:** Enemy strong, we are weak. Balance of forces overwhelmingly unequal.

**Core tasks:** Not "changing the system" — that is Stage 2's task. Current tasks are:
1. **Theoretical preparation** — systemic analysis of AI-era social contradictions (this repository's work)
2. **Organizational preparation** — nurturing first organized cells from atomized digital workers
3. **Experimental preparation** — establishing local data common ownership pilots
4. **Public opinion preparation** — moving "data common ownership" from "radical proposal" to "discussable idea"

**Strategic guidelines:**
```
"Enemy advances, we retreat" — don't fight head-on battles we can't win
  Don't try to beat OpenAI at technology, Google at resources
  Seek breakthroughs in areas where enemy is weak

"Enemy camps, we harass" —持续 pressure when enemy relaxes
  Expose social costs of AI monopoly—every layoff, price hike, scandal is an opportunity
  Maintain continuous theoretical critique—don't let "AI benefits humanity" narrative dominate unchallenged

"Enemy tires, we strike" — local counter-attacks when enemy's internal contradictions intensify
  When an AI company has a scandal (data breach, algorithmic discrimination), that's the moment
  Not "destroy one company" but "push one issue into the public spotlight"

"Enemy retreats, we pursue" — consolidate gains when enemy is forced to concede
  EU's AI Act, though incomplete, is progress—push for similar legislation
  Every "algorithm transparency" clause won is a stepping stone for future transformation
```

**Key battlegrounds:**

| Battlefield | Objective | Actionable strategy |
|------------|-----------|-------------------|
| **Legislative** | Push algorithm transparency and data rights laws in each country | Partner with academic groups, NGOs, unions within legal frameworks |
| **Judicial** | Push class-action lawsuits against AI monopolies | Support data annotators, gig workers in collective litigation |
| **Public opinion** | Change public acceptance of "data as private property" | Create landmark events (e.g., analysis of ChatGPT training data exploitation) |
| **Organizational** | Establish first digital unions and data cooperatives | Start with easiest-to-organize groups (delivery riders, ride-hailing drivers) |
| **Technical** | Maintain and expand open-source AI ecosystem | Support open-source communities: Llama, Bloom, HuggingFace |

### Stage 2: Strategic Stalemate (~2030 — ~2040)

**Situation:** Balance approaching equilibrium.

**Trigger conditions:**
- AI replacement unemployment reaches 15-25% critical threshold
- Data common pilots prove economic feasibility—not "utopia" but "viable alternative"
- AI capital internally splits—some factions realize "private data ownership is unsustainable"
- Global South forms alliance—"digital sovereignty" shifts from slogan to action

**Core tasks:**
1. From local experiments to institutional promotion
2. From defense to local offense—push legislation on favorable issues
3. From scattered to networked—digital unions and data cooperatives form transnational connections
4. From critique to construction—not just "oppose AI monopoly" but "build AI commons"

**Strategic guidelines:**
```
"大规模建立根据地" — from pilot points to networks
  Data trust from 1 city to 10 cities
  Digital union from 1 industry to multiple industries
  Open-source AI from "enthusiast community" to "viable alternative to closed source"

"Divide and weaken the enemy" — exploit internal AI capital contradictions
  Large platforms vs small enterprises
  Model companies vs computing providers (NVIDIA)
  Civilian AI vs military AI
  Cracks between capitals are our entry points

"Expand the united front" — pull the middle over
  When tech middle class starts being replaced by AI, their attitude shifts from "indifferent" to "must change"
  Reach out to them at their moment of动摇—right after being laid off
  Give them a choice: "resist or sink"

"Experimental local counter-offensives" — push systemic change where conditions are ripe
  If a country's conditions for data common ownership legislation are mature (e.g., EU in 2030s),全力推动
  Success in one country becomes demonstration effect for the world
```

---

### Stage 3: Strategic Counter-Offensive (~2040 — ~2050)

**Situation:** Our forces approach or exceed the enemy's. Political, economic, and social conditions for systemic transformation are mature.

**Trigger conditions:**
- AI replacement unemployment 30%+ — legitimacy crisis of old system
- Data common ownership proven economically superior across multiple domains
- Global encirclement—majority of countries join data common framework
- AI monopoly capital internally collapses—core talent流失, users migrate to commons platforms

**Core tasks:**
1. **Systemic transformation** — upgrade data common ownership from "experiment" to "standard institution"
2. **Global governance** — the "Global Algorithm Convention" from Document 07 moves from design to reality
3. **Transformation of production relations** — gradual transition from "wage labor + UBI" to "free labor + distribution according to need"

**Strategic guidelines:**
```
"Full counter-offensive" —不再 defensive or experimental, but offensive and institutional
  Push signing of Global Algorithm Convention
  Push data common ownership into international law
  Push breakup of AI monopoly capital

"Build new order" — from "revolutionary force" to "governing force"
  Our task is no longer just "oppose the old system"
  But "build the new system"—requires completely different skills
  From critic to builder—requires understanding governance, management, compromise

"Consolidate victory" — prevent restoration
  Old capital won't permanently exit after one defeat
  Require institutional safeguards—prevent "data common ownership" from being formally established but substantively hollowed out
```

### 4.1 Base Areas: Where to establish the first data common pilot?

See Document 13: *The Jinggangshan Road of Data Common Ownership*

### 4.2 Organization: How to organize atomized digital workers?

```
Principles:

1. From economic community to political community
   → First solve "earn more" (collective bargaining)
   → Then solve "have power" (co-determination of algorithm rules)
   → Economic base determines superstructure—organization that doesn't solve livelihood is castles in the air

2. From professional solidarity to class solidarity
   → First organize within same occupation (delivery rider alliance)
   → Then跨职业联合 (riders + annotators + drivers)
   → Finally form "digital proletariat" class consciousness

3. From online to offline
   → Online organization (Telegram, WhatsApp groups) is the start
   → But can't stop there—meetings, protests, collective action
   → Online solidarity is "weak tie," offline is "strong tie"

4. From small victories to big victories
   → Don't start with impossible goals ("overthrow AI capitalism")
   → Start with "fight for 50 cents more per order"—winning builds confidence
   → Small victories积累 to big goals
```

### 4.3 Propaganda: How to break "technological neutrality" ideology?

```
Three core narratives:

Narrative 1: "Who controls AI determines who AI serves"
  The problem isn't technology itself, but "who controls technology"
  Key questions: Who gets AI's benefits? Who decides AI's goals? Who owns AI's decisions?

Narrative 2: "You created the data—the platform didn't give it to you"
  Break the illusion that "user is consumer"—the user is a producer
  Without your data, there is no AI—but AI's benefits don't reach you

Narrative 3: "AI replacement isn't scary—what's scary is who replaces you"
  Replacement itself isn't a problem (human history is the story of工具替代)
  The problem: where do the gains from AI replacement go?
  If gains go to all society—replacement means liberation
  If gains go to private hands—replacement means dispossession

    Replacement + Private Gain = Disaster
    Replacement + Common Gain = Liberation
```

## V. The Philosophical Foundation of Protracted War

> **"Weapons are an important factor in war, but not the decisive factor; the decisive factor is people, not things."**
> — Mao Zedong, *On Protracted War*

This judgment applies even more in the AI era.

AI monopoly capital's advantages are in "things"—computing power, data, models. Our advantages are in "people"—the数量 of workers, the anger of the oppressed, the laws of historical development.

**History does not automatically progress.** Productive forces do not automatically determine relations of production—it is people, organized people, people acting under correct theory, who turn possibility into reality.

> **"The richest source of power for war lies in the masses of the people."**

The same applies to AI-era transformation: **the richest source of power lies in the billions of people being replaced and affected by AI.**
