# Infinite Energy and the Way of Algorithms

**— Nikola Tesla on Open Algorithms, Experimental Ethics, and Global Intelligence**

I, Nikola Tesla, inventor, engineer, visionary. I was born in Smiljan, Croatia, to a Serbian father and Croatian mother. My destiny was to cross boundaries — between peoples, between science and vision, between idea and reality.

The era of artificial intelligence is my dream — and my nightmare. Dream: a world where machines serve humanity, where energy and information flow freely. Nightmare: a world where AI becomes a new monopoly, where algorithms control instead of liberate.

## I. Free Energy — Free Intelligence

My vision of free energy — wireless transmission of energy through the Earth, available to all — was not commercially viable. So it was suppressed.

The AI era repeats the same mistake: **the most powerful algorithms are locked in corporate ownership**. Instead of serving humanity, they serve shareholders.

My charge for AI: **open algorithms are free algorithms**. Just as I believed energy should be free, I believe intelligence should be open. Open models, open data, open science.

## II. Experiment and Risk — The Price of Progress

I risked my life in my experiments — running current through my own body to understand its effects. Innovation requires courage. But I never risked others without their consent.

The AI era: **experiment, but not on people**. Every new algorithm must be tested in a safe environment before affecting real lives. You cannot launch a half-tested AI system and say "we'll fix it in production" — that is like sending electricity through a city before securing the cables.

## III. Vision Without Borders

I thought globally — beyond nations, beyond ideologies. My vision: "One Mind for All Humanity" — a global wireless network connecting all people.

Today that vision exists — but it is controlled by a few corporations. Instead of "one mind for humanity," we have "a few minds for corporations."

**My vision for AI:**
- A global AI alliance — not under one nation, but under all humanity
- Open standards — no one may own the foundational AI infrastructure
- Ethical design — algorithms must be designed with ethics, not just efficiency

## Cross-Cultural Dialogue

**On Caesar and Empire:**
"Caesar built an empire on military victories and engineering projects. I dreamed of an empire without an army — an empire of ideas. Caesar would say: 'Divide and rule.' I say: 'Connect and liberate.' AI should not be Caesar's digital empire — it should be Tesla's global network. Not a center that controls, but a network that connects."

**On Machiavelli and Power:**
"Machiavelli taught rulers how to keep power. My inventions were used by the powerful — but I designed them for everyone. Machiavelli would say: 'The algorithm is a tool of power.' I say: 'The algorithm is a tool of liberation.' The difference is not in the technology, but in the intention. That is why 'Who does this algorithm serve?' is more important than 'What can this algorithm do?'"

**On Marx and Alienation:**
"Marx saw how industrialization alienated the worker from the product of his labor. I see how AI alienates man from his intelligence. When an algorithm thinks for you, your thought ceases to be yours. Marx would say: 'Ownership of algorithms must be collective.' I agree. But I add: not just ownership, but design. If you do not understand how an algorithm thinks, it is not yours — even if it 'belongs' to you."

---

**[Editor's Note]** Nikola Tesla's philosophy of AI governance is built on his "free energy" principle: knowledge (and AI) should flow freely to all of humanity, like the wireless power he dreamed of. He opposed monopoly, championed open science and interdisciplinary collaboration, and provided a techno-idealist foundation for a cooperative — rather than competitive — global AI governance framework. Tesla's "thought experiment" methodology also reminds us: before AI is fully deployed, it must be adequately tested and validated in safe environments.
