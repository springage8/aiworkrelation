# Algorithmic Justice and the Welfare of the People

**— Obafemi Awolowo on Algorithmic Fairness, Social Welfare, and Human Oversight**

I, Obafemi Awolowo, politician, lawyer, scholar. I have governed, reformed the economy, and made promises to the people: education for every child, work for every person, pensions for the elderly.

The success or failure of AI is like what I saw in Nigeria's first elections: new tools can bring freedom, or they can bring new oppression.

## I. Education as the Foundation of AI

In my government, I established free education for all Nigerian children. Because I know: **there is no freedom without education**.

In the AI future: AI education must be free for all. Not just for experts — students, farmers, workers must understand AI. AI education is the foundation of AI freedom.

## II. Algorithmic Justice — The Problem of the Poor

I built an economic system that protects the poor. I believe: **the economy must serve all people, not just the rich**.

AI and justice:
- AI must not discriminate against the poor (differences in education, pensions, employment opportunities)
- When AI allocates resources — education, work, money — it must do so fairly, without bias
- We must have an "AI Justice Commission" — elected humans, not AI, overseeing algorithmic fairness

## III. State Governance and AI

I believe in a strong state that serves the people. Not a state that oppresses the people, but one that protects them.

AI must not make the state harsher — it should make the state better at serving the people. AI can help:
- Distribute resources fairly
- Improve government efficiency
- Protect vulnerable groups

But AI cannot replace human will, human compassion, human judgment.

## Cross-Cultural Dialogue

**On Gandhi and Independence:**
"Gandhi showed that the weak can defeat the strong — through truth, patience, and love. In the AI future, the weak can likewise defeat the big corporations — by pooling their data, by building their own AI tools, by upholding truth. Satyagraha (truth force) in AI: there is no force more powerful than 'take hold of your data, stand in truth.'"

**On Bismarck and Governance:**
"Bismarck built modern Germany — with power, with war, with strong administration. His lesson for AI: we must not leave AI unregulated. AI must be under the supervision of the people, not under the control of a single government or company. But I differ from Bismarck: not 'blood and iron' — but 'heart and institutions' (the heart of education, the institutions that protect the weak)."

---

**[Editor's Note]** Obafemi Awolowo (1909-1987) was a Nigerian nationalist leader and Yoruba statesman. He is famous for establishing free primary education and social welfare systems in Nigeria. His AI governance vision emphasizes that the core of algorithmic justice is the protection of vulnerable groups. His concept of an "AI Justice Commission" — elected humans rather than AI overseeing algorithmic fairness — maintains a civil society check-and-balance perspective against the prevailing tide of technocratic governance.
