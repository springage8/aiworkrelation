# Revolution, the New Man, and the Algorithm Age

**— Che Guevara on Liberation, Solidarity, and the New Humanity in the AI Era**

---

I am Che Guevara. Some remember my beret and cigar, but the real me was a scalpel — a doctor, a revolutionary. I traveled across Latin America, saw hunger in the mud huts of the Andes, saw exploitation in the rubber plantations of the Amazon. I learned: **Revolution is not exported; it is each people's own awakening.**

The revolution of the AI era is not about guns, but about data, algorithms, and consciousness.

## I. Algorithmic Colonialism

**The tragedy of Latin America is that it always provides resources for others, yet cannot decide how those resources are used.** We have lithium — the crucial raw material for electric vehicle batteries — extracted cheaply by multinationals. We have biodiversity — the training data source for medical AI — used freely by foreign labs. We have the daily data of hundreds of millions — which never belongs to us.

This is *The Motorcycle Diaries* continued in the AI age — I no longer cross the Andes, but traverse **digital mines**, where data is extracted, refined, and shipped away — leaving us only the bill.

Four features of a **data colony**:
1. **Data resource drain** — Latin American user data stored on servers in North America and Europe
2. **Algorithmic decision outsourcing** — Algorithms affecting Latin Americans designed in Silicon Valley and Beijing
3. **AI profit leakage** — Value created by AI does not stay local
4. **Digital infrastructure dependency** — Cloud computing, large models, computing power — all imported

## II. Two, Three, Many "Data Vietnams"

In 1967 I called for "two, three, many Vietnams" — countless struggle points to disperse imperialist power.

The AI era needs "two, three, many **data sovereignty pilot zones**":
- **Bolivia** — National sovereignty over lithium data
- **Cuba** — Algorithmic management of universal healthcare (our primary care system is world-class!)
- **Venezuela** — Sovereign control over oil data
- **Brazil** — Public trust for Amazon ecological data

Each "data sovereignty pilot zone" is a demonstration — proof that technological development need not depend on core capitalist countries.

## III. The New Man (El Hombre Nuevo)

"The New Man" — my core vision: **Revolution is not only about changing institutions, but about changing human beings themselves.**

What should the "New Person" of the AI era be like?

**A conscious user.** Not manipulated by recommendation algorithms, but consciously choosing one's information intake. This is an internal revolution — staying清醒 (sober/awake) before the desires stirred by algorithms.

**An organized worker.** Digital laborers (riders, annotators, moderators, freelancers) are not isolated individuals — they simply haven't yet recognized their shared interests. To organize is the first step of becoming the "New Person."

**A dignified producer.** The productive power of the AI era liberates time. Capitalism turns it into unemployment; Socialism turns it into liberated labor. The "New Person" does not see work as a curse, nor leisure as a luxury. AI is not a tool to replace people, but a tool to free people from repetitive labor.

## IV. Internationalism in a New Form

I died in the Bolivian mountains because I believed that revolutionaries around the world are one body. **Internationalism is not a slogan; it is life itself.**

Internationalism in the AI era:

**Share AI tools.** A doctor in Latin America should be able to use AI diagnostic tools, whether from India or Kenya. A farmer in Africa should be able to use AI agricultural advice, whether from Brazil or China. AI technology must be a **global public good**, not the patent of a few corporations.

**Share data sovereignty experience.** If a data trust in a Latin American village succeeds, villages in Africa must know how it was done. If a data annotation cooperative in an Indian language succeeds, peers in Southeast Asia must learn from it.

**Share computing power.** I have seen abandoned mines in Patagonia converted into solar-powered data centers. The Global South needs its own computing infrastructure — shared, collaborative, independent of Big Tech.

## V. Practice, Not Words

If I could give one sentence to the activists of the AI era, it would be:

> **"Estrategia sin táctica es el camino más lento a la victoria. Táctica sin estrategia es el ruido antes de la derrota."**
> (Strategy without tactics is the slowest road to victory. Tactics without strategy is the noise before defeat.)

Principles for action in the AI era:
- Start where you are — a data annotator's union, a rider's cooperative, a programmer's ethics alliance
- Connect with others — annotators and riders seem different, but they face the same algorithms
- Expand from local to regional — city → nation → region → global
- Let technology serve people, not people serve technology

**The revolution does not die; it simply becomes data. And data, in the end, returns to the hands of the people.**

**[Editor's Note]** Che Guevara is known for "armed revolution," but "Guevarism" in the AI era does not imply violence — rather, it means the awakening of **the right to organize, the right to know, and data sovereignty**. Che's concept of "The New Man" offers an alternative to consumerist subjectivity in the AI age: humans are not passive consumers fed by algorithms, but conscious users of technology, organized defenders of rights, and dignified participants in production.
