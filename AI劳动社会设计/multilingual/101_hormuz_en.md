# Oil Tankers Storm the Strait of Hormuz — A Dialogue Between Khomeini Jr. and Trump on the Middle East

> Spring 2026. Iranian oil tankers charge toward the Strait of Hormuz, just as during the Iran-Iraq War, as U.S. forces mass in the Middle East. Mojtaba Khamenei (Khomeini Jr.) and Donald Trump engage in a dialogue spanning war, peace, divine revelation, and nuclear clouds — until even the voices from heaven can no longer remain silent.

---

**Dramatis Personae:**

**Khomeini Jr.** — Mojtaba Khamenei, born 1969, the third Supreme Leader of Iran. At 17, he joined the Revolutionary Guard to fight in the Iran-Iraq War, later trained as a mid-ranking cleric at the Qom Seminary. For three decades he never held public office, yet was always his father Ali Khamenei's "gatekeeper" — WikiLeaks called him "the power behind the robe." In February 2026, an American-Israeli airstrike killed his father and wife. Three weeks later, the Assembly of Experts appointed him Supreme Leader. He almost never speaks in public — but when he says "no," no one in Iran says "yes."

**Trump** — Donald Trump, born 1946, 45th and 47th President of the United States. A real-estate mogul, Wharton graduate, author of *The Art of the Deal*. He has gone through four bankruptcies and four comebacks. Brash and direct, he sees everything as a deal. 2026 is the final year of his second term — an old man who knows the clock is ticking, facing a middle-aged man who has just lost everything.

**Netanyahu** — Benjamin Netanyahu, born 1949, Prime Minister of Israel (1996-1999, 2009-2021, 2022-2026). Holds dual degrees from Harvard and MIT Sloan; former Sayeret Matkal special forces captain. The longest-serving prime minister in Israeli history, elected three times, once forced out by corruption charges only to stage a comeback. For thirty years, his political through-line has never changed — "the Iranian nuclear threat" — and no one has yet proven him wrong. 2026 is his final term: an old man who has survived cancer, in the twilight of his political life, making one last gamble for his nation's survival. He will not accept any deal he believes endangers Israel — and he has ways of making Washington listen.

## Round 1: Shouting Across the Void

**Khomeini Jr.:**

(He does not open like an orator. He opens like a man who has been pacing a room for a long time and finally sits down.)

Old man, you're back. My father is gone — you bombed him. My wife is gone — you bombed her too. I sit here without the fire of revenge — I sit here with a question: You killed my father and my wife, then you say "let's talk" — how do I talk?

But I came. Not because I wanted to — because in thirty-seven years of my father's rule, Iran was never this humiliated. American-Israeli airstrikes, the Supreme Leader killed, a third of the Revolutionary Guard's command wiped out — and then you call me?

But to be honest, old man — at least you talked to us. You tore up the JCPOA, but at least you sat across the table. The ones who came after you — sanctions, sanctions, sanctions. Sanctions are not a policy. Sanctions are a fig leaf for doing nothing.

So you tell me — now what?

**Trump:**

(Pause — no evasion, no apology.)

Your father — was a target. Bibi pushed for three years, I gave the go-ahead in February. Your wife… that happens in war. Not personal to her — personal to your command structure.

Soleimani was my call alone. Publicly. Your father — a US-Israel joint operation. I don't apologize for war decisions.

(Waiting for Khomeini to speak. But he does not speak immediately.)

**Khomeini Jr.:**

(A long silence — then he speaks, his voice so calm it does not belong to someone whose family's murder has just been admitted — perhaps precisely because it is too calm.)

You admitted it. But your words hid another name — "Bibi pushed for three years."

(Pause.)

My father waited his whole life to hear an American admit it honestly. Not "collateral damage" — not "intelligence failure." He said "US-Israel joint operation" — he died before he got that honesty.

(Pause.)

Where is Netanyahu right now? Would he say the same things to my face?

(Another pause.)

Continue. You have more to say.

**Trump:**

Alright. Private feelings can't affect a deal. You hate me? Fine. We still have to talk. The cost of not talking is higher.

Now look — oil at $126. People talk about the "Trump Peace." Right. Because I made everyone scared. Saudi Arabia is scared, so they pay. Iran is scared, so they don't move. That's the art of the deal. Fear is the best leverage.

*(A voice that belongs to neither man — descending from above, from outside, from between them all at once. Not an echo, not a hallucination — a voice that lands on the page like solid ink.)*

**God of Israel:**

I made a covenant of blessing with Abraham. You make deals out of fear with your adversaries. The chips on your table are not numbers — they are children's names, they are cities' names, they are the future of entire civilizations. You call this "a good deal"?

I said to Abraham: All nations on earth will be blessed through your offspring. Not "will possess land" — "will be blessed." Blessing is the descendants of Ishmael and Isaac eating at the same table. Your oil tankers and aircraft carriers — in My eyes, they are ants fighting over a grain of sand.

Continue. I am still listening.

**Khomeini Jr.:**

(Silence — not from fear, but from being interrupted. Then he recovers.) Did you hear that?

**Trump:**

Hear what? It's just us in here.

You ask me what to do? Two options. One: bring me back. The moment I'm back, everyone sits at the table — not because I'm kind, but because I don't play by the rules. Two: you fight. I don't care. Iran's missiles hit Dubai — and I still have to put out the fire. That's how history works.

But that tanker suicide rush of yours — clever. Not clever militarily — that's a joke — clever in the media. Every camera on the Strait of Hormuz. You turned "suicide" into leverage. I'll use that move myself.

At the end of the day, we're the same. Only difference: I admit I'm making deals. You pretend you're acting on principle.

(Voice from the depths of the desert — cracked with three days of thirst.)

(Simply.)

## Round 4: Technology Bypass

**Trump:**

You know, I could make the Strait of Hormuz an ordinary waterway in three months.

Not with warships — with technology.

The UAE's Habshan-Fujairah pipeline can move 1.5 million barrels a day — already bypassing the Strait. Saudi Arabia's Petroline East-West pipeline — 5 million barrels — also bypassing it. Iraq's Kirkuk-Ceyhan pipeline — to Turkey's Mediterranean coast — theoretically 1.6 million barrels a day. It's been bombed, but it can be repaired.

We are talking to Oman about floating production storage and offloading units — FPSOs — loading oil right outside the Strait of Hormuz. Deployed in three months. Your tanker rush is useless — buyers don't need to go through the Strait to get their oil.

Your real nightmare is the Iran-Jask pipeline — from Bandar Bushehr around the Strait of Hormuz to Jask Port on the Gulf of Oman. A plan Iran itself has been floating for ten years. $2 billion. Build it and your oil goes straight out to the Gulf of Oman — Hormuz has nothing to do with you anymore. So why hasn't it been built? Because the Revolutionary Guard's military bases on the western coast of Hormuz live off "Strait crises" — no crisis, no budget. People on your own side don't want it built.

**Khomeini Jr.:**

(Silence.)

You know about the Jask project. Yes, it has been shelved. Not for technical reasons — for political reasons. Blockading the Strait is our biggest negotiating chip. Build the Jask pipeline, and that chip is gone. And — you are right — the Revolutionary Guard's bases on the western coast of Hormuz live off this card.

But there is another option you didn't mention — Iraq's alternative route. Baghdad exports over 3 million barrels a day through Hormuz. If Iraq builds a pipeline to Syria's Mediterranean coast — or through Jordan to Aqaba — Iraq can bypass the Strait too. I have spoken to Baghdad — they are also afraid of Hormuz being blockaded, because their export channel is as vulnerable as mine.

**Trump:**

Iraq-Jordan pipeline? My people assessed it. From Basra to the port of Aqaba — 1,800 kilometers. Through desert full of tribes — ISIS territory. Cost at least $12 billion. Five years. Politically, harder than climbing heaven.

But you are right about one thing: people on your side don't want the Jask pipeline built. The ones who live off crises — they need the Strait of Hormuz to remain the "world's jugular."

So take this as advice from me: Build the Jask pipeline. Once it's built, your oil goes out through the Gulf of Oman. Hormuz has nothing to do with you anymore. You no longer need to threaten blockade — but you are also no longer afraid of being blockaded. That's what the Chinese call "advance and retreat, both secure" — you think I only know real estate negotiation. I know military strategy too.

**Khomeini Jr.:**

You are teaching me strategy?

Building the Jask pipeline requires $2 billion, three years, imported steel, foreign technology — all choked off by your sanctions. Your advice sounds good, but it depends on you letting me buy steel.

But — you at least said something useful. If the Jask pipeline is built, the Strait of Hormuz is no longer Iran's jugular — it becomes the world's jugular. **This is a different kind of card: not "I can choke you" — but "you can no longer choke me."**

Let's continue talking. **But remember: the way to convince me is not to scare me — it is to give me a way out.**

## Round 5: Nuclear Reality

**Khomeini Jr.:**

Israel has at least 90 nuclear warheads — submarine-launched, air-launched, Jericho missiles that can reach Tehran. We have 40 years of enrichment experience, a Natanz that has been bombed several times, a pile of centrifuges, and a slogan about "peaceful nuclear energy."

My people ask a very simple question: Why can Israel have nuclear bombs — and we cannot?

You answer.

**Trump:**

Because we are winners — and you are losers. That's how the world works.

But… fine. Real answer: Israel built them first — they started in 1948, when you were still in the middle of your revolution. Timing is everything. They had nukes while you were still burning American flags.

Now you say you want them too. Of course you do. Anyone surrounded by enemies with nukes would want them. It's a rational choice.

The question is not "do you want them" — the question is what happens after you get them. Saudi Arabia wants them. Turkey wants them. The UAE — maybe them too. The entire Middle East becomes a nuclear supermarket. Then one miscalculation — boom.

**Khomeini Jr.:**

So what is your solution? Give me a reason not to build nuclear weapons — convince me. Don't just hand me a blank page.

Peaceful nuclear energy + IAEA inspections. Every centrifuge tagged. Fordow near Qom — 90 meters underground — do you know why it was built underground? Because you bombed it three times, and it is still there.

Let me tell you my real position: I can publicly say "we accepted the most stringent inspections ever" — that is a concession on my part domestically. But I cannot publicly say "we will never seek nuclear weapons" — because the people will ask: Why can Israel have them?

So here is the solution — **a Middle East Nuclear-Weapon-Free Zone.** Not Iran renouncing nuclear weapons — everyone renouncing them together. Israel, Iran, all countries. IAEA inspections + satellite monitoring + transparency mechanisms. Saudi Arabia too.

That sounds fair. You let me go back to the Supreme Leader — I am the Supreme Leader now — and tell the Iranian people: We renounced nuclear weapons because everyone renounced them. I can say that.

You get Netanyahu to tell the Israeli people: We renounced nuclear weapons. If he can — I will bow. If he cannot — then Iran will not renounce them either.

**Trump:**

You want me to go to Netanyahu and say "hand over your nukes." You're crazy.

(Pause)

But you're right. Fair is fair.

I won't push for a nuclear-weapon-free zone — because Israel won't agree, AIPAC would kill me, and Congress would blow up. But I'll tell you a real solution: **Don't call it a "nuclear-weapon-free zone" — call it a "Nuclear Stability Framework."**

## Conclusion: The Spiral Staircase

**Editor's Note:**

Six rounds of dialogue, four divine interruptions, one apocalyptic vision, three patriarchs' discourse (Cyrus, Maimonides, King), one intrusion of reality from Jerusalem, one round of negotiation cornered by technology alternatives.

The most interesting turning point occurred in **Round 4: Technology Bypass** — when Trump laid out the full technological hand of the Habshan-Fujairah pipeline, the Petroline, the FPSO floating terminal, and the Kirkuk-Ceyhan alternative route, Khomeini fell silent. Not because he was convinced — but because he could not refute it: the Strait of Hormuz was indeed being bypassed by technology. Iran's greatest negotiating chip was not military power — it was geography. And geography was being rendered obsolete by technology.

Round 5's nuclear showdown was equally pivotal — Netanyahu's forceful intervention tore the dialogue from a "U.S.-Iran bilateral game" into a "three-way game with Israeli veto power." He refused to be excluded from any negotiation touching Israel's security, forcing Trump to admit in front of Iran that "deals need Jerusalem's approval" — a stunning admission in itself. But Trump used a single phrase — "Israel retains the right to act independently" — to hand the initiative back to Netanyahu, forming a fragile triangular balance.

At the same time, both sides faced an honest truth for the first time: Iran's pursuit of nuclear weapons is a rational choice, not ideological madness. The conversation shifted from "should you or shouldn't you" to "if you have them, what do we do?" This is the language of truly pragmatic diplomacy.

They did not reach an agreement. No handshake. No historic "Trump-Khamenei Accord." They did only three things: acknowledge each other's red lines, understand each other's real positions, and agree to talk again.

And six rounds ago — they were threatening to bomb each other back to the Stone Age.

**Progress is sometimes not from hatred to love — it is from hatred to "I hear you."**

Placing these solutions on a timeline:

```
[Now] → Tanker standoff → Crisis management mechanism (6-12 months) → Jask pipeline construction (1-3 years)
  → Nuclear stability framework negotiations (3-5 years) → Regional security architecture (5-10 years) → Civilizational dialogue (10-20 years)
```

This is not a "plan" — it is a direction. No step depends on the success of the next step — but each step makes the next step easier.

Finally, two improbable events that history might overlook:

1. **Iran-Israel covert cooperation** — In recent years, there have been reports that Iran and Israel maintain an "invisible hotline" in Syria — neither side is willing to admit it, but both use it to avoid direct conflict. If secret communication channels between enemies already exist — perhaps the "solution" is not to create new ones, but to use what is already there.

2. **Oil price collapse** — If global energy transition accelerates (electric vehicles proliferate, shale oil production rises, renewable energy breakthroughs), oil could drop to $40/barrel within five years — the strategic significance of the Strait of Hormuz would naturally die. This variable is not controlled by any party — but may be the real antidote.

**The ultimate irony of realpolitik:**

Khomeini Jr. — a man whose father and wife were killed in an airstrike — needs the America he hates to continue existing as a threat, in order to keep the Revolutionary Guard and hardliners rallied around him. It is not that he does not want peace — it is that the position he sits in does not allow him to choose peace.

Trump — a man approaching eighty, who has survived assassination, impeachment, conviction, and bankruptcy — needs the narrative of "I can fix everything" for history to remember him. He does not want the problem to disappear entirely — because a world that does not need him, does not need him either.

Netanyahu — an old man who has staked his entire political life on the "Iranian nuclear threat" — faces the same paradox: if the agreement succeeds, he is proven to have been raising a false alarm; if the agreement fails, he is proven powerless to stop it. Every minute he sat in that corner was the embodiment of this paradox — he did not come to negotiate. He came to ensure that no agreement could either succeed enough to make him redundant, nor fail enough to make him responsible.

They all need the other to continue existing — which is precisely what they hate most about each other.

In this sense — the Middle East does not need to be "solved." The Middle East needs to be managed — with patience, with rules, with economic interdependence that makes war unprofitable. Because **history's progress is never a straight line** — it is a spiral staircase. Every time you turn a corner, you rise a little higher, even if you appear to be facing the same direction.

**The one thing you cannot do is stop turning.**

The oil tankers are already on the Strait of Hormuz. But as long as there is dialogue — even a round of rude, selfish, boastful dialogue — war has not yet won.
